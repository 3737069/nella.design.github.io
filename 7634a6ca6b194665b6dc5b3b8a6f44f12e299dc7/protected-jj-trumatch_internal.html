TRUMATCH™ surgeon portal MVP

YEAR – 2021, 4 months
ROLE – Digital Experience Designer @ Johnson & Johnson
STATUS – Launched in 2021

TomoFix is a patient-specific medical device used in knee surgery that reduces surgery time and improves patient outcome. However, the ordering process involves a lot of paperwork and is overall a clunky experience.

The goal is to digitalize the end-to-end service to better serve surgeons and improve work efficiency for the China market.

Working in a cross-functional team, I was responsible for defining user flows and prototyping key features of the TRUMATCH™ surgeon portal MVP. I also created a UI style guide and worked with the developers in China to ensure the platform comes to life. The result is a shortened lead time from 10 days to 5 days and improved user satisfaction.

Problem

While TomoFix has its benefits of shortened surgery time and improved patient outcome, the patient-specific instrumentation (PSI) journey introduces added complexity. It involves additional stakeholders, paperwork, and communication across teams. Surgeons may choose conventional methods over PSI due to a lack of trust in the existing process.

Constraints

  • Delayed immersion - Before the J&J design team onboarded the project, a 2-month contextual research had been conducted by an external service design agency, which meant we had to quickly bridge knowledge gaps and catch up on prior insights.
  • Small UI/UX team - On the UI/UX design side, it was mainly just me and my direct manager. There was a separate brand team working on the branding in parallel.
  • COVID - Due to COVID travel bans, we had to work remotely between China and Singapore. We also had limited time directly with surgeons due to their demanding schedule, so feedback was gathered in a presentation-style format by our China team rather than through longer collaborative sessions.

New task flow

We worked on designing the surgeon task flows closely with the business and development team.


The key features prioritzed for the MVP are:

  1. Home dashboard
  2. Case intake form
  3. Design review and approval
  4. Case management

1. Home dashboard

The surgeons' mindset when they enter the platform is rarely to browse or explore but to get things done efficiently. To facilitate their workflow, the primary components of the dashboard are:

  • Create new case button
  • Cases pending action table
  • On-going cases
  • Learning resources

2. Case intake form

Submitting the intake form used to be a tedious and manual process, the digitalized form provides a more seamless and secure experience. It also eases workload on our internal sales team.

3. Design review and approval

Once the engineers have completed the cutting guide design specifications, it would be submitted for the surgeon to review and approve.

4. Case management

A record of on-going and past cases for reference. Users can easily filter and search for cases.

Reflections

It was my first time working with the China team and it challenged me to adapt to a fast-paced environment, the key was to have frequent check-ins to make sure expectations were aligned.

Another key take away was to design the home dashboard last, by focusing first on the core flows, it became easier to design a functional home dashboard that meets the surgeons' needs.
